OBiBa was launched in 2007 as a core project of the Population Project in Genomics (P3G). The OBiBa project grown rapidly and obtained substantial financial support from its main partners to build new open source software applications for data collection and management. In 2012, OBiBa and the DataSHaPER project, also from P3G, joined their efforts to create the Maelstrom Research program whose mission is to develop tools and methods for epidemiological data harmonisation.

Maelstrom Research offices are located at the Research Institute of the McGill University Health Center, Montreal, Quebec.

Our Software

OBiBa software consists on a suite of performing stand-alone applications that support particular studies activities. The applications can be customized and integrated to create a comprehensive information management and analysis system for individual studies. Moreover, as part of the Maesltrom Research program, our suite integrates advanced software components enabling data harmonization and federation for study networks that aim to share securely data among their members. OBiBa software applications are easy to use and are built upon the latest programming technologies. Its architecture is specially designed to ensure that participant privacy and data security are fully respected.
